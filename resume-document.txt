 3. Structural Dissipation Process 
 
 3.1 Core Innovation 
The true innovation of the SCM system is access to all three 
locations and the structural dissipation methodology applied to 
the data. 
 
 3.2 Detailed Dissipation Algorithm 
 
 Step 1: Analyze the Target Structure 
- Look at the dimensions of your target tensor (e.g., 4x3 
matrix) 
- Identify the "importance" or "weight" of each position based 
on its structural role: 
  - Corner positions get higher weight (they touch fewer 
neighbors) 
  - Edge positions get medium weight (they touch some 
neighbors) 
  - Interior positions get lower weight (they touch many 
neighbors) 
 
 Step 2: Assign Structural Weights 
- Corners: Weight = 3 (most isolated, highest concentration) 
- Edges: Weight = 2 (moderately connected) 
- Interior: Weight = 1 (most connected, lowest concentration) 
 
 Step 3: Calculate Total Weight Budget 
- Count positions: 2 corners + 4 edges + 6 interior = 12 
positions 
- Calculate weight sum: (2×3) + (4×2) + (6×1) = 6 + 8 + 6 = 
20 total weight units 
 
 Step 4: Distribute Value by Weight 
- Base value per weight unit: 5 ÷ 20 = 0.25 
- Corners get: 3 × 0.25 = 0.75 each 
- Edges get: 2 × 0.25 = 0.50 each 
- Interior gets: 1 × 0.25 = 0.25 each 
 
 Step 5: Identify Unique Edge Signatures 
After dissipation, scan the perimeter and catalog the unique 
value patterns: 
- Type A edges: Corner positions containing 0.75 
- Type B edges: Non-corner edge positions containing 0.50 
- Transition points: Where 0.75 meets 0.50 (corner-to-edge 
boundaries) 
 
 Step 6: Edge Uniqueness Detection 
- Trace around the perimeter clockwise 
- Record the sequence: 0.75 → 0.50 → 0.50 → 0.75 → 0.50 
→ 0.50 → 0.75 → 0.50 → 0.50 → 0.75 
- Identify unique patterns: 
  - Peak edges: Values of 0.75 (corners) 
  - Valley edges: Values of 0.50 (regular edges) 
  - Gradient zones: Transitions between peak and valley values 
 
 3.3 Result 
The original concentrated value 5 is now unevenly spread 
according to structural importance, creating a natural 
landscape where corners hold more value than edges, and 
edges hold more value than interior points. The unique edge 
signature becomes a fingerprint of how the dissipation 
respected the tensor's geometry.
